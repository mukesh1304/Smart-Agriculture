1. Land Preparation

The land is plowed, harrowed, and leveled for proper corn seed germination and growth.

2. Seed Selection

Choose hybrid or drought-resistant corn seeds based on local climate and soil conditions.

3. Sowing

Seeds are planted at the right depth and spacing, often using planters for uniform distribution.

4. Irrigation

Proper watering is crucial, especially during germination and the flowering stages of corn.

5. Fertilization

Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizers and organic compost to boost corn growth and yield.

6. Weed and Pest Management

Use herbicides early and monitor for pests like corn borers; apply eco-friendly treatments as needed.

7. Harvesting

Harvest corn when kernels are hard and husks turn brown; can be done manually or with harvesters.

8. Post-Harvest Handling

Dry corn to safe moisture levels, shell, and store properly to avoid mold and pests.
