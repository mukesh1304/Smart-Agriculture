1. Land Preparation

The land is plowed and leveled. Puddling (flooding and tilling) is commonly done to prepare the rice field for planting.

2. Seed Selection and Treatment

High-quality seeds are selected. Sometimes seeds are treated with fungicides or soaked to encourage sprouting.

3. Nursery Preparation (for Transplanting)

Seeds are first grown in a nursery bed. After 20‚Äì30 days, seedlings are ready for transplanting into the main field.

4. Transplanting or Direct Seeding

Seedlings from the nursery are transplanted into the field, or seeds are directly sown depending on the farming method.

5. Irrigation

Rice requires continuous water supply. Fields are kept flooded during much of the growing period to ensure healthy growth.

6. Fertilization and Weed Control

Fertilizers (organic or chemical) are applied to promote growth. Regular weeding ensures nutrients are not stolen by other plants.

7. Pest and Disease Management

Use of natural methods or approved pesticides to protect the crop from insects and diseases.

8. Harvesting

When the grains turn golden-yellow, rice is harvested either manually or with machines.

9. Post-Harvest Processing

Rice is threshed, dried, and stored. Milling may be done to remove husk and polish the grains.
