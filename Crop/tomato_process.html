1. Land Preparation

Prepare well-drained fertile soil by plowing and adding compost to improve structure and nutrients.

2. Seed Selection and Sowing

Choose high-yielding, disease-resistant tomato varieties and sow seeds in a nursery for healthy seedlings.

3. Transplanting

Transplant healthy seedlings into the field after 25-30 days, maintaining proper spacing for growth.

4. Irrigation

Water the plants regularly, especially during flowering and fruit development stages, without waterlogging.

5. Fertilization

Apply nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers at critical stages to enhance flowering and fruit setting.

6. Pest and Disease Management

Monitor for common diseases like blight and pests like fruit borers; use eco-friendly pesticides and treatments.

7. Harvesting

Harvest tomatoes at the breaker stage (first sign of red color) for best shelf life and flavor retention.

8. Post-Harvest Handling

Sort, grade, and store tomatoes carefully to avoid bruising and maintain freshness during transport.
